===========================================================
               DebriSense Demo Presentation
===========================================================

[Slide 1]
1.0 About
- DebriSense is an intelligent, map-based debris monitoring and reporting platform.
- It unifies public citizens, NGOs, and environmental administrators into a single, cohesive ecosystem to report, track, and strategically analyze river debris.
- The platform provides a centralized, interactive command center for preserving river health.

[Slide 2]
2.0 Why DebriSense?
- Current mitigation of river debris is often fragmented, relying on slow, outdated, or disconnected manual reporting systems.
- Without a centralized, data-driven dashboard, authorities struggle to predict debris accumulation or allocate cleanup resources efficiently.
- DebriSense bridges this gap by combining crowdsourced community tracking with predictive analytics, making river mitigation proactive rather than reactive.

[Slide 3]
3.0 Objectives
- To develop a real-time river monitoring system that updates hotspot debris status at defined intervals.
- To develop a predictive Debris Risk Index (DRI) model that generates debris accumulation prediction at fixed time intervals and evaluates hotspot emergence with quantifiable accuracy metrics.
- To implement an interactive chatbot that provides instant guidance as well as context-aware answers, tailored to stakeholder’s needs.

[Slide 4]
4.0 Features
- User Account Management: Tiered accessibility for System Admins, verified NGOs, and standard Public Users.
- Real-Time River Monitoring: Interactive GIS mapping with personalized Watchlist capabilities.
- Predictive Debris Risk Index: Data-driven (DRI) modeling to automatically calculate and highlight critical debris hotspots.
- Interactive Chatbot: An integrated AI assistant to guide users and provide contextual river health answers seamlessly.
- Report Management: Comprehensive workflow allowing users to submit Hotspot Reports with photo evidence, and for Admins to review and export the data.

[Slide 5]
5.0 Proposed Solutions
- Community Crowdsourcing: Empowering both Public and NGO stakeholders to easily report localized trash sightings directly via map pins, multiplying the eyes on the ground.
- Automated Intelligence: Relieving administrative burden by letting the DRI automatically calculate risk factors and flag locations needing immediate cleanup attention.
- Streamlined Administration: Offering a centralized dashboard for Admins to perform advanced Hotspot Analysis and moderate user-submitted reports effortlessly.

[Slide 6]
6.0 What's Next
- Mobile Application Expansion: Launching native iOS and Android apps for faster, on-the-go reporting in the field.
- Hardware/IoT Integration: Connecting physical river sensors and AI cameras to fully automate debris imaging and tracking.
- Geographical Scaling: Expanding the monitoring network beyond the current pilot rivers to encompass nation-wide environmental tracking.
