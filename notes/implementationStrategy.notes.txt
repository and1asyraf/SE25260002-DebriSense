The implementation strategy for DebriSense adopts a hybrid development approach, beginning with a prototype-first philosophy before transitioning into structured iterative cycles guided by the Agile methodology. This strategic choice was made to ensure modular construction, continuous improvement, and adaptability throughout the 34-week project lifecycle. Most importantly, it allows for progressive development with regular stakeholder involvement, particularly incorporating feedback through bi-weekly review sessions with the project supervisor.

The development begins with a comprehensive requirement analysis and the construction of an early, simplified prototype. This prototype focuses on validating essential functionalities, such as hotspot visualization on the interactive map, the basic Debris Risk Index (DRI) computation flow, user authentication, and rudimentary debris reporting forms. By adopting this prototype-first approach, the core system architecture is validated early, allowing for the early detection of issues within the debris prediction logic and user interaction flow before complex features are layered on top. Once the prototype stabilizes and key assumptions are verified, the project transitions into structured iterative development cycles.

Implementation proceeds from the foundation level, focusing on the creation of robust backend services, database schemas, and API integrations. The system is decomposed into five core, manageable components: User Account Management Module, Real-Time Monitoring Module, DRI Prediction Module, Interactive Chatbot Module, and Report Management Module. These functionalities are developed independently to support modularity and ease of testing, with unit testing conducted early to confirm the correctness and stability of each module's foundation.

Following the prototype phase, the iterative development cycle is driven by Agile methodology, structured into two-week sprints resulting in 17 focused iterations. Each major system module is assigned specific sprint cycles to ensure organized and incremental development. For example, the DRI Prediction Module iteratively refines its rule-based logic and risk weighting methods, while the Report Management Module iterates on submission flows and validation rules. This ensures the system remains aligned with real-world requirements and stakeholder expectations, as supervisor feedback is integrated consistently into future sprint plans.

The final phase involves comprehensive integration testing of the entire system, where individual functionalities like real-time data ingestion, chatbot interactions, and predictive analytics are assembled into complete system modules. This is followed by thorough system testing to validate overall performance, reliability, and adherence to functional requirements. This hybrid approach not only optimises the development process by reducing unnecessary rework, but also ensures that DebriSense remains robust, user-friendly, and capable of supporting diverse stakeholders such as NGOs and administrators in real-world river debris management.
