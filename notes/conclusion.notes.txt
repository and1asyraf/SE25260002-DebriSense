6.0 Conclusion

In conclusion, the implementation of DebriSense successfully fulfilled the project’s objective of developing an integrated, predictive, and data-driven platform to monitor and forecast river debris accumulation in Penang. By leveraging Adaptive Knowledge Engineering and real-time environmental APIs, the system accurately calculates the Debris Risk Index (DRI) and forecasts debris type breakdowns, allowing timely and proactive cleanup logistics. The modular design, comprising the User Account Management, Real-Time Monitoring, DRI Prediction, Interactive Chatbot, and Report Management modules, ensures system scalability and maintainability. The system is accessible via a highly responsive web-based platform, providing real-time interactive updates and actionable insights to NGO field staff and environmental administrators.

The initial phase of the project, which focuses on non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and local administrators in Penang, demonstrates the immense potential of DebriSense to modernize environmental monitoring through real-time geospatial visualisation and predictive analytics. The development of this web platform shows the system’s ability to collect, process, and dynamically analyse river data effectively. The application facilitates real-time reporting of actual debris payloads, interactive map monitoring, AI-assisted guidance, and automated risk notifications, empowering users to take proactive actions. DebriSense proved to be fit for purpose through rigorous system testing, which confirmed that all modules operated successfully according to the defined functional and non-functional requirements. The adoption of a hybrid development strategy—starting with a prototype-first approach and transitioning to Agile methodology—further enabled flexible development and iterative enhancements, contributing to a well-rounded and efficient river management solution.

Additionally, the project has successfully achieved its core objectives by developing a user-centric system that integrates dynamic empirical feedback loops for debris forecasting. The system provides real-time visualisation of debris risk levels across various rivers through an intuitive and interactive dashboard. It generates reliable payload forecasts using real-time weather parameters combined with baseline land-use data, and dynamically corrects itself over time using historical cleanup reports. The functional design ensures that NGO staff can easily input ground-truth data, manage personalized watchlists, and receive instant automated alerts directly from the field. Integrating these automated notifications and empirical reporting features allows for rapid logistical responses to severe debris threats, thereby preventing critical river blockages and flooding. These features fulfill the project’s overarching goal of creating a predictive, community-driven environmental monitoring solution tailored specifically to Penang’s waste management needs.

While the current system delivers exceptionally on its intended functionality, there are several areas that offer opportunities for further development. A key area of improvement lies in the direct integration of physical Internet of Things (IoT) sensors. Currently relying on external macro-level weather APIs, deploying physical smart sensors directly into the rivers would provide hyper-localized data, drastically increasing the accuracy of the DRI calculations. Another significant enhancement would be transitioning the heuristic predictive engine into a fully trained Deep Learning model once sufficient years of NGO historical data have been aggregated. To expand the applicability of the system, future efforts should focus on integrating baseline data from additional regions beyond Penang, which would allow for broader deployment across Malaysia. Another priority is developing a native mobile application with offline functionality to support NGO fieldwork in remote areas with poor internet connectivity.

Looking ahead, the vision for DebriSense is to evolve into a nationwide platform for comprehensive solid waste monitoring and aquatic pollution management. With the potential for replication in other states or countries facing similar river plastic pollution issues, DebriSense stands as a scalable solution grounded in data-driven decision-making. Future development cycles will emphasize enhancing AI analytics, deploying hardware sensors, and incorporating automated policy-oriented reporting to better support government agencies and large-scale environmental stakeholders. Stakeholder engagement, continuous collaboration with active NGOs, and feedback from end-users will remain essential in refining the platform. As DebriSense continues to grow, its role in shaping sustainable river management practices, preventing urban flooding, and promoting ecological resilience in Malaysia will become increasingly vital.
